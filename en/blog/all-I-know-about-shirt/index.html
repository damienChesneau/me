1- The fabric

Like for suits, with the shirts fabrics finesse we have the fineness of the yarn which is taken into account for its quality, so we can have a notation like 50/1, 80/2, 140/2 the first one qualify the fabric finesse and the second number qualify the number of twists, that is the number of times the thread is twisted on itself.

Because in reality, more the finesse of the thread is high more the shirt will be fragile. So we twist the thread multiple time on itself, tow times for a “double twists” to ensure that this really fine thread will even be durable. There is even the triple twists, on high-end shirts. That gives also a shirt thicker and therefore less transparent.

Of course the higher the numbers are, more the price of the fabric will also be too. The interesting value for a good compromise is around 100/2-120/2.

Poplin: One of the most common fabrics for formal shirts, Its name comes from the papal city of Avignon where the fabric was invented. It can be made of wool, cotton, silk and even polyester. This fabric is totally smooth.

Fil à Fil: This is simply a variation of the poplin with a yarn of different colors to give another shade to the fabrics.

Oxford is thicker than poplin and has a smoother feel. the thread of this fabric is woven two by two. This fabric with two variations the classic and pinpoin the latter is thinner and therefore lighter.

Twill is a fabric easily recognizable by its oblique stripes it is a very simple fabric ironing, which crumples little and also very opaque. The thread is shifted to each row which gives these stripes. There is a declination which is the chevron twill which consists of alternating rows of stripe.

Linen, this is the natural fiber from culture. It is a natural fabric that is a good insulator in the winter and very breathable in the summer. This fabric is rather suitable for hot weather. The disadvantage is only that it creases automatically, so you have to know appreciate this feature and have good ironing skills.

The Giro Inglese is the most interesting fabric when it starts to get hot. Indeed its simple weave, is ventilated which allows the air to pass between. It is a fabric that is rather strong, durable and easy to iron.
